Orientation

Start here

This manual assumes you have never used the system before, and that you are accountable for it — not merely a user of it.

MAESTRO-SMS is the single place your school's records live: students and their families, classes and timetables, attendance, grades and report cards, fees and payments, staff employment and payroll, and the approvals that govern all of it. Everything is tied to one school — yours. Staff at another school on the same platform cannot see your data, and you cannot see theirs. That separation is enforced in three independent layers of the system, not by a setting anyone can toggle.

Two ideas explain most of how the system behaves, and if you take nothing else from this manual, take these:

You see only what your role permits. The menu down the left-hand side is not the same for you as it is for a teacher or a parent. Items are not greyed out — they are simply absent. So when a member of staff tells you "I can't find it," the usual answer is that they don't have that role, not that the system is broken.

Anything that moves money or grants power needs two people. A large payment, a refund, a salary change, a published grade, appointing a junior administrator — one person requests, a different person approves. This is deliberate. It protects your staff as much as it protects you, because it means no single person can ever be solely blamed for a disputed transaction.

Where to get help

The app has a built-in Help page that adapts to whoever is signed in — a teacher sees teacher guidance, an accountant sees finance guidance. Send staff there first. This manual is the leadership view that sits above it.

Your position

Owner vs. Principal — who controls what

The system does not have a role literally called "owner". Proprietors are normally set up as school administrator, and heads of school as principal. The two overlap heavily but are deliberately not identical, and understanding the split saves a great deal of confusion later.

ResponsibilitySchool administratorPrincipal
Configuration — settings, branding, integrationsYesNo
Managing roles and staff accessYesNo
Subscription, plan and billingYesYes
Approving fees, refunds and salary changesYesYes
Final stage of the staff approval chainNoYes
Academic oversight — grades, publishing resultsYesYes
Releasing exam answers after a testNoYes

The rule behind the split: the administrator configures the system, the principal operates the school. A principal should not be able to quietly change a setting that weakens a control they are also the final approver for.

Deliberate design

If you are both proprietor and head of school, ask to hold both roles — this is normal and supported. But do not hold both and expect to approve your own requests. The system will refuse: every approval stage must be decided by a different person from the one who raised it. Where a school genuinely has only one senior person, appoint a second trusted approver before you need one, not during the emergency.

Two roles you will never see

There are two platform-level roles — super administrator and manager administrator — that belong to MajorGBN, not to your school. They are invisible in your role list and cannot be created or assigned by anyone at your school, including you. They exist so the platform team can support you; every action they take in your school is written to an audit log you can read.

Day one

Your first login, and keeping the account safe

When your school is provisioned you receive a sign-in email containing a set-password link, not a password. The link is single-use and expires after seven days. If it lapses, use Forgot password on the login page rather than asking for the link again.

Set up two-factor authentication immediately

Go to Account and enrol an authenticator app. Your account is the most valuable one in your school — it can move money and grant access. Do this before you invite anyone else, so you can honestly ask them to do the same.

Once your own is working, consider turning on Admin → Roles → Require two-factor authentication for staff. It applies to every staff member and none of your students or parents. Staff who have not enrolled are held on their Account page until they do — they are not locked out, so it will not strand anyone mid-lesson.

If your school has exactly one administrator and that account locks, you must contact us to recover — WhatsApp is fastest for this. Appoint a second administrator on day one. It costs nothing and removes the single most common way schools lock themselves out.

Implementation

The first 30 days

This order is not arbitrary. Each step depends on the one before it — students cannot be enrolled into classes that don't exist, and fees cannot be invoiced to students who haven't been imported.

Week 1 — People and structure

  1. Create your staff accounts

    Admin → Users, then Admin → Roles

    Add teachers, your accountant, HR staff and your second administrator. Each receives a one-time temporary password and must set their own at first login. Assign roles as you go — a member of staff with no role can sign in but will see almost nothing.

  2. Import your students

    Admin → Bulk import

    Upload a CSV. The import is safe to re-run — it matches on email address, so a repeated upload updates rather than duplicates. Parent links can be imported in the same pass, which saves considerable work later. On approval the system generates printable login slips with a unique one-time password per student.

  3. Build classes, subjects and the academic session

    Classes

    Create each class, assign its teachers, and name a class supervisor (form teacher) — that person writes the class remark on report cards, so don't skip it. Enrol students, link guardians, then set up your session and its terms. Grading has nowhere to record marks until terms exist.

Week 2 — Timetable and fees

  1. Define periods and rooms, then place lessons

    Timetable

    Double-bookings are rejected as you go — the same teacher, room or class cannot be scheduled twice in one period. If you would rather not build the grid by hand, set how many lessons per week each subject needs and use Generate: the system solves the timetable for you and tells you precisely which lessons it could not place and why.

  2. Build your fee catalogue and issue invoices

    Fees

    Create your fee items, then issue invoices. Register the bank account fees should settle into. Parents can pay online by card immediately; the money reaches your account, not MajorGBN's.

Week 3 — Public face and examinations

  1. Brand your portal

    Admin → Branding

    Upload your logo (square, between 128 and 2048 pixels) and set your school colour and font. It appears for every member of your school and on generated documents — report cards, receipts, certificates and ID cards.

  2. Review incoming admissions

    Admin → Admissions

    Applications from your public application page arrive quarantined — an applicant is not a student and can see nothing until you admit them. If you charge an application fee, paid and unpaid status shows on each application.

  3. Schedule examinations

    Exams

    Create a sitting with hall, date, time and capacity; seat an entire class in one action; roster invigilators. Students and parents then see their own hall, time and seat number, and invigilators see their duties — which removes an enormous amount of exam-week correspondence.

Week 4 — Governance and delegation

  1. Walk the guardrails once, deliberately

    Admin → Audit, Security, Recertification, Privacy

    Open each of the four. You do not need to act on anything — you need to know they exist and what each is for before the day you urgently need one. They are described in full under The safety rails.

  2. Appoint a junior administrator

    Admin → Roles

    This is the single highest-value delegation available to you. A junior administrator handles day-to-day operations — records, attendance, timetable, recording fee payments, reviewing admissions — while every approval power stays with you. See Delegating.

  3. Confirm your subscription

    Billing

    Check your plan, your student count and your renewal date before your trial ends. Details under Money out.

Two shortcuts worth learning on day one

The search box in the header jumps straight to a student, staff member, class or invoice without navigating to the module first. For a principal answering a parent on the phone, this is the fastest route to an answer that exists in the system.

Approvals is your queue. If you check one page each morning, make it this one — it is where everything waiting on your decision collects, and where work quietly stalls if you don't.

Why a colleague's menu looks different

Two independent things decide whether a menu item appears:

  • Your role — whether you personally are permitted to use that area.
  • Your school's plan — whether your subscription includes that module at all.

If an entire area is missing for everyone including you, it is the plan. If it is missing for one person only, it is their role. That single distinction resolves most "it's not there" questions without a support call.

The junior administrator, specifically

This role deserves separate attention because it is the one that will most reduce your daily workload. A junior administrator can maintain records, take and correct attendance, adjust the timetable, manage documents, record fee payments, and review admissions. They cannot manage roles, cannot approve fees, and cannot sit in any approval chain.

In other words: they can do the work, but they cannot authorise it. That is precisely the shape you want for a capable office administrator you do not yet want holding financial authority.

Requires a second approver

Appointing a junior administrator — or adding any further role to someone who already is one — raises an approval request that a different senior must confirm under Approvals. You cannot approve your own appointment. This is intentional: quietly promoting someone is the classic route to an internal fraud, and requiring a second signature closes it.

Two protections on your own role

  • You cannot remove your own administrator or principal role. This prevents an accidental self-lockout.
  • The last senior role in a school cannot be removed. Your school can never end up with nobody in charge.

When someone leaves

Revoke the account under Admin → Users. Access ends immediately — not at their next login. Any session they currently have open stops working on their very next action, and any unused invitation link they hold becomes invalid.

Do not delete staff records. Employment history, payroll entries and audit trails are statutory records, and the system deliberately retains them. Revoking access and retaining the record is the correct outcome, and it is what an auditor will expect to see.

Governance

The safety rails

These are the features that separate a school management system from a spreadsheet. Understand them once and you will trust the numbers the system gives you.

Two people for anything that matters

The system enforces separation of duties in code, not policy. The person who requests can never be the person who approves — even when they hold both roles.

ActionRaised byApproved by
Payment of ₦50,000 or moreWhoever records itA different member of finance staff
Any refund, of any sizeWhoever records itA different member of finance staff
Fee discount or waiverWhoever requests itA different approver
Salary changeHRPrincipal, administrator or HR manager
Payroll run finalisationWhoever created the runA different person
Appointing a junior administratorAn administratorA different senior
Publishing gradesA teacherPrincipal or administrator
Staff leave and requestsThe member of staffHead → HR manager → Principal

Payments below ₦50,000 post immediately — small routine receipts are not held up. It is the large ones and every refund that wait, because those are the two shapes financial fraud actually takes.

Audit — the record of who did what

Admin → Audit is a searchable log of every sensitive action taken in your school: who did it, to what, and when. Every reading of a student's medical record and every change to money or access is written here, and it cannot be edited or deleted by anyone — including MajorGBN.

Use it when a parent disputes a payment, when a mark appears to have changed, or when you simply want to know who altered something. It is also the fastest way to resolve an accusation between staff, because it replaces recollection with record.

Temporary access, instead of permanent over-permissioning

When someone needs access they do not normally hold — covering an absent colleague, say — Admin → Security grants it temporarily. It must be approved by a different person, it expires automatically, and every use of the elevated access is logged.

The alternative most schools reach for is granting a permanent role "just for now" and forgetting to remove it. Over a few years that is how everyone ends up with everything.

A limit that also protects you

Temporary elevation can never grant platform-level or two-person-rule permissions. Nobody can use it to escalate themselves to approving their own refunds, managing roles, or reaching another school's data. The most sensitive permissions in the system are simply not obtainable this way.

Access review

Admin → Recertification lists who currently holds what, and flags anything unusual. Work through it once a term. It takes twenty minutes and is the only reliable defence against permission creep.

Data protection requests

Admin → Privacy handles the two rights families may exercise under NDPR: a full export of the data you hold on them, and a request for erasure. Erasure is reviewed rather than automatic, because some records — financial and statutory ones — you are legally required to keep. The system distinguishes the two for you.

Finance

Money in: school fees

The flow is: build a catalogue of fee items, issue invoices from it, and let payments settle against them. An invoice moves through draft, issued, partially paid, and paid on its own as money arrives — you never set the status by hand.

How parents pay

  • Card, online — the parent pays from their own portal. Funds settle to your registered bank account.
  • Bank transfer — each student can be issued a dedicated account number. A transfer into it is matched to the student and applied to their oldest open invoice automatically.
  • Cash or in-person — recorded by your accountant, subject to the ₦50,000 two-person rule.

Every posted payment sends a receipt to the payer, the guardians and the student, showing the remaining balance. Numbered PDF receipts can be downloaded at any time.

Payment plans and credit

An invoice can be split into instalments that must sum exactly to the total. The plan itself never moves money — each tranche simply reads as paid, due, overdue or upcoming based on what has actually been received.

Families can also pay in advance. Prepayments sit as a credit balance on the student and are applied to future invoices. Overpayments become credit automatically rather than needing a refund.

Discounts, waivers and scholarships

Discounts and waivers are requested and then approved by someone else, and the approved amount can never exceed what is actually outstanding. Separately, MajorGBN runs a platform-funded scholarship programme — a parent or teacher applies on a student's behalf, guardian consent is required, and an award is paid directly against that student's invoice at your school.

Chargebacks and disputes

If a parent disputes a card payment with their bank, it appears under Fees → Disputes with the deadline by which evidence must be submitted. Your finance staff are alerted automatically. Treat these as urgent: an unanswered dispute is normally lost by default, and a pattern of them puts your ability to accept card payments at risk.

What to watch, and where

Fees → Reports gives you receivables ageing and collection rates. Overdue reminders go out weekly on their own, and late fees — if you configure them — are applied once per invoice, automatically. There is also a journal export for your accountant or auditor.

Commercial

Money out: your subscription

This is what your school pays MajorGBN, and it is entirely separate from the fees you collect from parents. It lives under Billing.

How you are charged

Pricing is per active student, multiplied by your plan's rate and the length of cycle you commit to. Committing further ahead costs less:

CycleCoversDiscount
Monthly1 month—
Per term3 months5% off
Per year9 months (three terms)15% off

Note that a "year" is the academic year — three terms, nine months — not twelve calendar months. It is priced to match how schools actually budget.

Plans

There are four tiers — Standard, Premium, Ultimate and Enterprise — each including everything in the tier below. Your plan decides which modules exist for your school at all; roles then decide who inside your school can use them. If you need a single module from a higher tier rather than the whole tier, ask support to enable it individually.

Renewal, and what happens if you lapse

A banner warns your billing staff from 14 days before renewal. You can save a card for automatic renewal, which is attempted about two days before expiry.

Your data is never at risk

If a subscription lapses, access to higher-tier modules is suspended after a grace period — core teaching functions continue. Your data is never deleted, and paying restores full access immediately. A billing problem should never become an academic emergency, and it does not.

Upgrading, and paying for growth

Upgrade at any time — unused time on your current plan is credited against the new one, so you are not charged twice. If your student numbers grow mid-cycle, you are asked for a top-up covering the additional students for the remainder of the cycle only.

Referrals

Under Billing you can generate a referral code. When a school you refer takes their first paid subscription, both schools receive a free term. For a proprietor with peers running other schools, this is the cheapest way to reduce your own cost.

If you run more than one school

Proprietors with several campuses can add the Group console, which shows aggregate figures across all of them in one view. It deliberately shows numbers only — never student records — so a group-level view never becomes a route around each school's data separation.

Operations

The academic core

Attendance

Teachers take a daily register per class. Marking a student absent or late notifies their guardians automatically — which is both a safeguarding measure and, in practice, the feature parents notice first.

Grades and report cards

Teachers enter marks for their own classes. Results are weighted per term, and publishing them requires approval — a teacher cannot release results to parents alone.

Report cards are generated as PDFs from actual grades and attendance, carry your branding, and include two written remarks: one from the class supervisor and one from the head. Each generated card is filed automatically into the student's document vault, so families can retrieve it later regardless of who produced it.

Examinations

Two separate things share the word "exam":

  • Exams — the logistics of physical examinations: halls, dates, seat allocation and invigilator rosters.
  • CBT exams — computer-based tests sat in the system. The clock is enforced by the server, correct answers are withheld until a sitting closes, and answer release is gated to the principal. Automatic marks are presented as figures for staff to review, never as a final result.

Cover for absent teachers

Under Timetable → Teacher cover, choose a date range and the system lists every lesson whose teacher is on approved leave. Assign a reliever and they are notified; the system refuses anyone already teaching in that period. This closes the gap where an approved leave request and an unattended class are tracked in two different places.

Academic integrity

For written assessments the system can detect signs of copying and contract cheating — pasted text, unusual typing patterns, similarity between submissions. Two things are worth stating plainly to your staff and your parents:

  • It produces signals for a teacher to judge, never a verdict. No student is ever penalised automatically.
  • Monitoring is disclosed, consent-gated and time-limited, and individual students can be exempted where an accessibility need requires it.

Being able to say this confidently to a concerned parent is part of why it is built this way.

Operations

Staff, parents and communication

HR and payroll

HR holds employment records. Salaries are encrypted, and every read of them is logged — including the list view. Payroll runs snapshot each active employee's pay, calculate PAYE and pension, and must be finalised by someone other than whoever created the run. Payslips are available as PDFs, and there is a bank transfer export.

The module also covers recruitment, onboarding and offboarding checklists, document expiry reminders, appraisals, disciplinary case files, and final settlement when someone leaves.

Leave

Any member of staff applies under Leave. The request travels the approval chain, and on approval the balance is decremented automatically. Half-days are supported. Approved leave feeds directly into teacher cover.

Parents

A linked guardian sees their own children and nothing else: attendance, grades, invoices, documents, messages. This scoping is enforced by the system rather than by the interface, so a parent cannot reach another family's records by any route.

Meetings lets staff open appointment slots that parents book themselves, for their own child only. It removes the scheduling correspondence around parents' evenings entirely.

Reaching people

  • Announcements — one-way, to a group.
  • Messages — two-way conversations. Parents and students may only message staff, never each other.
  • Notifications — automatic alerts: absence, invoices, receipts, approvals waiting on you.

Email and in-app messages are unlimited. SMS and WhatsApp consume message credits, purchased in bundles under Billing. A credit is only spent once the message is confirmed sent — a failed delivery costs you nothing. Everyone can control which external channels they receive under their own Account page; essential notices such as receipts and invoices always get through.

Practice

Your operating rhythm

The difference between schools that get value from this system and schools that fight it is almost entirely habit. This is a workable one.

Each morning · 5 min

  • Clear Approvals — nothing should wait a day
  • Scan Notifications
  • Check attendance was taken

Each week · 20 min

  • Fees → Reports — collection and ageing
  • Any open disputes — these have deadlines
  • Analytics — attendance trend by class
  • Cover arranged for next week's absences

Each term · 1 hr

  • Recertification — who still needs access
  • Review the audit log for anything unfamiliar
  • Confirm results were published and cards issued
  • Check the subscription against real student numbers

Each year · half a day

  • Roll classes and the academic session forward
  • Archive leavers; add them to Alumni
  • Review your plan against what you actually use
  • Confirm a restore from backup has been tested
The one habit that matters most

Clear your Approvals queue daily. Almost everything that stalls in this system — a payment not credited, a teacher's leave unconfirmed, results not reaching parents — is waiting on a decision, not on the software. Five minutes each morning prevents nearly all of it.

Support

When something goes wrong

A member of staff says they cannot see a page

Check whether it is missing for you too. If it is missing for everyone, your plan does not include that module — contact us to enable it. If only they cannot see it, they lack the role: fix it under Admin → Roles. Have them sign out and back in afterwards, as permissions are read at login.

Someone is locked out

Three failed passwords locks an account permanently. An administrator reactivates it from the user's record. If it is your account and you have no second administrator, message us on WhatsApp — and appoint a second administrator once you are back in.

A payment does not appear against an invoice

Give it a few minutes; card payments confirm asynchronously. If it still has not landed, check whether it is held awaiting approval — payments of ₦50,000 and above wait for a second person. Failing that, a daily reconciliation sweep compares your ledger against the payment gateway and posts anything missing.

A parent says they cannot see their child

The guardian link is missing. Link them under Classes, on the child's record. This is the single most common data gap after a bulk import that did not include parent columns.

I approved something by mistake

Approvals are deliberately not reversible — that is what makes the record trustworthy. Correct it with a compensating action instead: for a payment, record a refund (which itself needs a second approver); for a role, remove it. Both the original decision and the correction remain visible in the audit log, which is the honest outcome.

The system feels slow

First establish whether it is slow for everyone or one person — a single slow user is almost always their network. If it is genuinely school-wide and sustained, tell us: capacity is adjustable, and slowness is a signal to act on rather than tolerate.

I need to know who changed something

Admin → Audit, filtered by date, person or record. Every sensitive action is there, and the log cannot be altered by anyone.
