## Part 1 — The problem with the traditional approach

Most Nigerian schools still run on some combination of paper registers, exercise books, spreadsheets, WhatsApp groups, and cash. It works — until it doesn't. Here is what it actually costs you:

### Money you never see

| Traditional practice | What it costs you |
|---|---|
| Cash and bank-teller fee collection | Leakage, "lost" receipts, reconciliation disputes, and no real-time picture of who owes what |
| Handwritten receipts | No proof trail; disputes resolve in the parent's favour |
| Arrears tracked in a notebook | Debts age silently; follow-up depends on one person's memory |
| Refunds and large payments handled by one person | The classic fraud vector in school finance |

### Time your staff burn

| Traditional practice | What it costs you |
|---|---|
| Manual result computation | Teachers spend evenings and weekends on arithmetic, with errors that damage credibility |
| Handwritten report cards | Days of work per term, per class |
| Paper attendance registers | No same-day visibility; chronic absence discovered too late |
| Manual timetabling | Clashes discovered on Monday morning |
| Payroll on spreadsheets | PAYE, pension, and NHF computed by hand — errors compound into statutory exposure |

### Trust you quietly spend

| Traditional practice | What it costs you |
|---|---|
| Parents in the dark between terms | Disengaged families, late interventions, and churn to schools that communicate |
| Exam malpractice handled ad hoc | Results your community doesn't fully believe |
| Records in cabinets | Fire, flood, or one disgruntled staff member away from disaster |
| No audit trail on anything | When something goes wrong, you cannot prove what happened |

The traditional approach is not free. It is the most expensive system you will ever run — you just never see the invoice.

## Part 2 — The MAESTRO-SMS solution

One platform, one login per person, seventeen tailored roles — from proprietor and principal to teacher, accountant, HR, hostel warden, driver, librarian, student, and parent. Every person sees exactly what their role permits, and nothing more.

### 2.1 Fee collection that pays for the platform

- **Online payment (card, bank, USSD via Paystack)** with settlement **split directly to your school's own bank account**. We never hold your money.
- Every posted payment generates an **instant digital receipt** to the payer, the guardians, and the student — with the outstanding balance on it.
- **Invoice lifecycle** (draft → issued → part-paid → paid) with receivables-aging and collection reports, so you know exactly who owes what, and for how long.
- **Maker-checker on money**: large payments and *all* refunds require approval by a second staff member. One person can never move money alone.
- **Overpayment alerts**, gateway refund push-back to the original card, and no hard-deletion of financial records — ever.
- **Paid admission forms**: charge for application forms online, with paid/unpaid tracking built into your admissions review.
- Dual currency support (Naira and USD) for international-facing schools.

### 2.2 Academics that run themselves

- **Term-weighted gradebook** with continuous-assessment slices pulled automatically from online quizzes and assignments.
- **Report cards as polished PDFs** — your logo, your branding — generated from grades and attendance, delivered to guardians automatically.
- **Grade publishing is governed**: a teacher's results go to families only after the head teacher *and* principal approve. No more "wrong result went home."
- **Timetabling with clash detection**: the system refuses double-booked teachers, rooms, or classes at entry time.
- **Attendance in seconds**: per-class digital registers; an absence or late mark automatically notifies the guardian *the same day*.

### 2.3 A real LMS — not a brochure feature

- Lessons, learning materials, quizzes, assignments, and moderated class forums.
- **Live classroom sessions** (Zoom, Google Meet, or Jitsi) with a join-window and an attendance register.
- Content versioning, reuse and cloning across classes; teacher-awarded achievement badges; per-class learning analytics.
- **Installable, offline-capable app** (PWA) — students on unstable connections keep learning.

### 2.4 Computer-based testing with real integrity governance

WAEC/JAMB-style CBT, built the way examinations should be run:

- Teachers build **question banks per subject they teach** — a Kahoot-style form for question entry, with bulk import for existing banks.
- Each student sits a **freshly sampled, shuffled paper**; the timer is enforced by the server, not the browser. Closing the laptop gains nothing.
- **Answer keys never reach a student's device during an exam** — technically impossible to leak from the browser, because the server never sends them.
- **Publishing an exam requires a second approver.** Releasing the answer key requires the **principal's** explicit approval. Marking is automatic; the numbers reach staff for review before anything becomes official.
- The separate **Assessment Integrity module** watches for paste-ins, tab-switching, and implausible typing on written work — and produces *signals for a teacher's human judgment*, never automated punishment. That is a policy stance: technology informs; educators decide.

### 2.5 Parents inside the tent

- A dedicated **parent portal**: children's attendance, results (once published), invoices and online payment, documents and report cards, school calendar, and two-way messaging with staff.
- Automatic notifications for absences, fee receipts, published documents, and announcements — in-app, and via email/SMS/WhatsApp where enabled.
- Engaged parents renew. Communication is retention.

### 2.6 Complete HR & Nigerian statutory payroll

- Staff records with **encrypted salaries**, contracts, document-expiry reminders, training records, appraisals, and disciplinary files.
- **Payroll that knows Nigeria**: PAYE tax bands, 8% pension, NHF — computed automatically, snapshotted per payslip, with bank-transfer CSV export and statutory remittance schedules.
- Salary changes are **maker-checker** (requested by one person, approved by another, history kept forever). Payroll finalisation requires a second person too.
- Leave management with staged approvals (head → HR → principal), duty rosters, staff attendance with an anti-spoofing kiosk mode and biometric-device support, staff loans with automatic payroll recovery, exit settlements, and official letters generated on your letterhead.
- A **recruitment pipeline** with a public careers page — applications flow straight into your review queue.

### 2.7 Operations: hostel, transport, library, discipline

- Hostel rooms and allocations (capacity-guarded), transport vehicles/routes/assignments, library catalogue and loans — each with its own scoped role (warden, driver, librarian) that sees only its own domain.
- A structured discipline module with case files, evidence, and append-only histories.
- Facility fee runs (hostel/transport) initiated by facility heads post **only after finance-level approval** — operations staff never move money alone.

### 2.8 Engagement your students will talk about

- **Ten educational games**, including live Kahoot-style quizzes hosted by teachers, typing races, chess, checkers, hangman, and the Dead & Wounded number-deduction family — duels, class races, elimination rings, leagues and knockouts with standings.
- **Cross-school championships** exist but are governed the way children's data must be: school opt-in *plus* per-student guardian consent, pseudonymous handles only — a student's real name never crosses to another school.
- Platform-sponsored **scholarship programs** with governed application, guardian consent, and qualification exams run on the CBT engine.

### 2.9 The approval engine underneath everything

Money, grades, exams, salaries, leave, content publishing — anything consequential moves through a **multi-stage approval workflow** with separation of duties enforced by the engine itself: the person who requests can never be the person who approves, and every stage is decided by a different individual. Every transition is written to an immutable audit log.

This is the governance layer that turns "software" into "an institution."

## Part 4 — Why MAESTRO-SMS beats other school management systems

You may have seen other school software. Here is what they typically are — and what we are instead.

### 1. Security that is architectural, not cosmetic

Most school systems check permissions in application code and hope for the best. MAESTRO-SMS enforces **three independent layers of isolation** — your school's identity is verified in the login token, re-checked by the application, and *enforced again inside the database itself* (PostgreSQL Row-Level Security). Even a software bug in the application cannot leak one school's data to another, because the database refuses to serve it. Cross-school probing doesn't even learn that a record *exists*.

Add to that: two-factor authentication, permanent lockout after repeated failed logins, forced password rotation, step-up re-authentication for sensitive actions, **field-level AES-256 encryption** for medical records and salaries (encrypted per-school, unreadable even in a stolen database copy), and just-in-time privilege elevation with approval and auto-expiry. This is banking-grade posture applied to children's data — because that is what the data deserves.

### 2. Built for Nigeria, not localized to it

Naira-first with kobo-exact accounting. Paystack settlement to your own account. PAYE bands, pension, and NHF in payroll. WAEC/JAMB-style CBT. NDPR-aligned consent, retention, and data-subject rights (export and governed erasure). Report cards and letters in the formats Nigerian schools actually use. Competing products bolt this on; we started here.

### 3. Governance, not just record-keeping

Other systems store data. MAESTRO-SMS **governs process**: separation of duties on money, grades, exams, salaries, and content is enforced by the platform's approval engine — not by a policy memo. For an owner, that is the difference between software and internal control.

### 4. An ethical spine on integrity and children's data

Our integrity tooling produces **evidence for human review — never automated punishment**. Cross-school play requires guardian consent and never exposes a child's real name. Monitoring is disclosed, retention-bounded, and audit-logged. Schools that care about their values can adopt technology without compromising them.

### 5. Depth that eliminates your software zoo

LMS + SIS + fees + CBT + HR/payroll + hostel + transport + library + admissions + messaging + analytics + games — **one platform, one bill, one login per person**, instead of five subscriptions that don't talk to each other and a spreadsheet to glue them together.

### 6. Engagement no competitor matches

No other school management system ships a live quiz arena, chess, typing races, and inter-school championships with consent governance. Students *want* to log in. That is adoption — the thing most school software fails at.

### 7. Proven engineering, not promises

The platform is verified by **hundreds of automated tests on every change** — including a dedicated test category that proves cross-school isolation for every single data table, and fails the build if a new table skips it. Production infrastructure is cloud-deployed with monitoring, structured logging, and error tracking. This is engineering discipline most vendors in this market simply do not practise.

## Part 7 — Frequently asked questions from owners

**Where does fee money go?** Directly to your school's bank account via gateway settlement split. The platform never holds your funds.

**What if we stop paying?** Your data is never destroyed and your plan is never erased. Access narrows to the core teaching tier after a grace period, and full access restores the moment payment lands. Financial and academic records are append-only throughout.

**Can our data leak to another school on the platform?** The database itself enforces school isolation on every row of every table — verified by automated tests on every release. Cross-school access attempts don't even learn a record exists.

**Do we need special hardware?** No. Any browser on any device. The student app installs like a mobile app and tolerates poor connectivity. Biometric attendance devices are supported if you have them — never required.

**What about our existing records?** Bulk student import is built in; our onboarding support covers migration of your current term's structure.

**Who else can see children's data?** Nobody without a role-based, audited reason. Medical records are encrypted at the field level and every access is logged. Integrity telemetry is retention-bounded and purged on schedule.
